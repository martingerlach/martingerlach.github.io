 I am a Postdoctoral Fellow at Northwestern University working in the group of [Luis Amaral](https://amaral.northwestern.edu/). Before that I got a PhD at the [Max Planck Insitute for the Physics of Complex Systems](https://www.pks.mpg.de/) working with [Eduardo Altmann](http://www.maths.usyd.edu.au/u/ega/). 

  
   

I am interested in understanding dynamics of complex social systems -- How do seemingly complicated macroscopic patterns emerge from simple rules determining the interaction of individuals, such as the spreading of new words in a community of speakers?
In order to address these questions I combine statistical analysis of large datasets of human activity (for example millions of books from the past 5 centuries or millions of scientific publications from the past 50 years) with mathematical tools from complex networks and machine learning.


My research interests include:

* Topic models and community detection in complex networks
* Production of scientific knowledge and evolution of scientific fields (Science of Science)
* Language change and spreading of linguistic innovations
* Statistical laws in linguistics and complex systems more generally
* Scaling laws of urban indicators (Science of Cities)
* Personality / Individual Differences
